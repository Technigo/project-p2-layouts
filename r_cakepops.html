Champagne Cake Pops

A bunch of tiny, bubbly party cakes!

  • 250 g butter
  • 200 g sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 300 g flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 150 ml champagne or prosecco or sparkling wine of your choice

  • 75 g butter
  • 100 g cream cheese
  • 150 g icing sugar
  • 50 ml champagne / prosecco / sparkling wine
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

  • 450 g white chocolate coating
  • 1 tsp coconut oil
  • sprinkles
  • Active: 1 h
  • Baking: 50 mins
  • Resting: 2 h
  • Total: 3 h 50 mins
  • Preheat oven to 180Â°C
  • Bake 50 mins
  • Beat the softened butter and the sugar with a hand mixer for 5 minutes at highest speed until creamy.
  • Add one egg at a time and mix in between.
  • In a second bowl mix the dry ingredients and sieve into the butter-egg mixture.
  • Stir everything again well, add champagne and stir briefly.
  • Pour in a greased cake pan and bake for 50 minutes. Let cool completely.

  • Beat the soft butter and cream cheese until creamy with a hand mixer. Add the remaining ingredients and stir until smooth.
  • Cut off the hard and dark parts of the pre-baked cake with a knife. Then take a large bowl and crush the cake into small crumbs with your hands.
  • Use your hands to mix the crumbs well with the frosting and form small balls (approx. 3-3.5 cm).
  • Let set in the fridge for 1 h before decorating.

  • Melt the chocolate coating in a water bath. If necessary, add 1 tsp coconut oil to smoothen the coating.
  • Dip a lollipop stick or wooden skewer finger-wide into the melted chocolate and stick into the cake pops.
  • Dip the Cake Pops into the chocolate coating until they are evenly covered. Let excess couverture drip off.
  • Add sprinkles and let dry.
  • Enjoy!
  • The Cake Pops are best kept in the refrigerator
  • Use a thick polystyrene plate as a suitable cake pop holder.
  • Enjoy a glass of bubbly on the side!
